Our founders

Seth Cohen

Co-founder

Seth believes there is tremendous power at the intersection of storytelling, community, and action. He works with leaders to help organizations turn community into a strategic advantage, designing ecosystems that connect people through shared purpose, knowledge, and curiosity. Drawing on more than 25 years across media, philanthropy, and civic leadership, Seth focuses on helping organizations cultivate communities that deepen trust, strengthen networks, and create lasting momentum.

Most recently he served as Chief Impact Officer at Forbes and founder of the Forbes Impact Lab, and before that as a Senior Director at Charles and Lynn Schusterman Family Philanthropies. Earlier in his career, Seth was a nationally recognized corporate attorney in Atlanta and has held numerous nonprofit leadership roles focused on civic engagement and community development.

Our approach

Applied Optimism.

Most organizations approach community the wrong way. They start with tools (platforms, events, newsletters, campaigns) and hope connection follows. But tools don't create community. People do.

Applied Optimism is our method for bringing people together and moving ideas forward. It rests on three things:

Purpose

People gather around something that matters.

Knowledge

Communities grow when people learn from one another.

Wonder

The best communities create energy, curiosity, and possibility.

When the three work together, community becomes more than engagement. It becomes momentum.
