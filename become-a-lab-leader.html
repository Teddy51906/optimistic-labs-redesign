The economics

You keep as much as possible. Full stop.

Lab Leaders earn a substantial percentage of direct client revenue, referral revenue for clients sent to other Labs, and profit-sharing in the platform as it grows.

Your existing contracts stay 100% yours, outside the platform arrangement entirely. New client work, renewals, and expansions are structured collaboratively during onboarding, with the goal of keeping as much income in your pocket as possible while sustaining the platform.

We're not taking a cut so you can use our logo. We're taking a cut because we're helping you sell, deliver, and grow.

Questions

Good questions, straight answers.

What exactly is a "lab"?
A lab is a productized practice serving a specific community or vertical. It has a clear methodology, packaged offerings and tiers, go-to-market support, co-branding, and Optimistic Labs' leadership and infrastructure behind it.
How is this different from just starting my own thing?
When you go alone, you're the practitioner and the entire operating apparatus; you're in charge of everything from brand, platforms, pricing, pipeline, back office, and it can be wildly overwhelming (and technical!). As a lab leader, you keep the mission, the expertise, and the community, and we carry the operational backbone to support it. It's independence without isolation; it's your practice, designed and supported, instead of built solo from zero.
Do I keep ownership of my brand and my community?
Yes. Your name, your voice, and your relationships stay yours and stay front and center. The co-branding adds the weight of the Optimistic Labs brand and trusted network on top of what you've already built; it doesn't replace it. (Exact ownership and partnership terms are designed per lab; we'll walk through them on the call.)
What do I actually get from Optimistic Labs?
Strategy and infrastructure behind you; a trusted network of operators, creators, and builders already in motion; help productizing and pricing your offerings; brand and credibility through the co-branded lockup; and go-to-market and demand generation. Plus partners who have built practices like this before.
What kind of person makes a strong lab leader?
An experienced practitioner or community leader with a specific audience, a genuine point of view, and the ambition to build a practice rather than a personal brand. If you've earned trust in a community, you already have the hardest part. Trust is the advantage. We help you build on it.
What happens after I book a call?
We talk through your idea, your community, and the lab you want to build. If it's a fit, we move into designing the lab together: methodology, offerings, positioning, and then launch with the OL apparatus behind you. There is no obligation to start a conversation with us; just bring your pitch and we can co-create from there.
