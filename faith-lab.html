Faith Lab is Optimistic Labs' practice for communities rooted in shared purpose. We partner with congregations, foundations, denominational bodies, nonprofits, and the leaders who carry them, to do the hardest, most important work a community can do: stay connected across real disagreement. We don't hand you a curriculum and walk away. We design and facilitate the experiences, relationships, and frameworks that let your community move through difference and come out more whole.

What we believe

Most organizations treat conflict as a problem to solve and difference as a risk to manage. We believe the opposite. Communities that learn to move through genuine disagreement, across ideology, theology, politics, and identity, don't just survive it. They become more trustworthy, more resilient, and more capable of living out the values they say they stand for.

The goal is not to broker agreement. It's mutual understanding and respect.

We're not here to broker agreement. We're here to build mutual understanding and respect — the kind that holds when the conversation gets hard. Faith, as we mean it, is what a community is rooted in: its traditions, its texts, its values, its shared purpose. Not a label, and not a fence. Every tradition already carries wisdom about how to hold difference, repair what's torn, and return to common ground. Our job is to translate that wisdom into tools you can actually use.

How we're different

A practice, not a playbook.

We design from the inside out

We don't separate logistics from programming. The room, the flow, the transitions, the moment someone walks in the door, all of it is part of the design. How a gathering feels is not an afterthought to what it's about. It's how the work actually lands.

We work toward our own obsolescence

We build capacity, not dependency. The point is to leave you able to keep doing this work without us, with practices and frameworks your own people can carry forward. Success means you need us less over time, not more.

We bring the outside in

A neutral perspective lets people see themselves and their community clearly, sometimes for the first time. The outsider can be a mirror, naming what's true in the room without an agenda inside the disagreement. That clarity is hard to reach from within.

We bring lived expertise inside

We're practitioners who know faith-based and values-driven organizations from the inside, not consultants reading from a manual. We understand the texts, the tensions, and the stakes, and we meet your community where it actually is.

Who we help

Three places this work begins.

Communities carrying an unaddressed conflict

Something difficult has gone unspoken, or a conversation keeps getting deferred because no one knows how to start it well. You need an experienced partner who can hold the room so the hard thing can finally be said, and heard. Let's talk about where you're stuck.

Mission-aligned organizations building programming

Foundations, denominational bodies, and nonprofits that want to help their broader communities navigate difference. You're not solving one conflict, you're building lasting capacity across many. Let's scope what you want to build.

Individual leaders growing their own capacity

Board members, clergy, educators, and practitioners who want to get better at this work themselves. You want sharper instincts, real practices, and a circle of peers doing the same. Let's find the right starting point for you.

What we do

Five ways we work with you.

Conflict Navigation & Difficult Conversations

When a hard conversation has gone unaddressed, or a community is reeling from a rupture, we design and facilitate the experiences that let people move through it together, with care and honesty.

  • The Facilitated Session — a single, carefully designed conversation to move through a specific moment of tension
  • The Community Building Process — a multi-session engagement for deeper, sustained work over time
  • Post-Crisis and Post-Launch Realignment — to help a community find its footing again after something hard or something big

Mission, Vision & Values Alignment

When what you say you stand for and what you actually do have drifted apart, we help you name the gap and rebuild the alignment, so your purpose shows up in your programming.

  • The Alignment Audit — an honest read on where mission, values, and lived practice line up and where they've come apart
  • The Programmatic Redesign — rebuilding how your programs work so they actually express what you stand for

Experience Design

Gatherings don't connect people by accident. We design the experiences, from the agenda to the architecture of the room, so the moments that matter most are built to land.

  • The Experience Design Consultation — where we shape the design of a gathering with you from the inside out
  • Event and Gathering Facilitation — where we lead the room so the experience does what you need it to do

Long-term Fractional Support

When community work needs a steady, senior hand over time, we embed as an ongoing partner, holding the strategy with you month over month rather than just for a single moment.

  • Strategic Community Advisor — an ongoing partner for the leaders shaping your community work
  • Fractional Chief Community Officer — senior community leadership on a fractional basis, in the room and in the strategy

Individual Practice & Development

This work is a practice, and practices grow with company and reps. We help individual leaders build their own capacity through cohorts, an ongoing circle, and one-on-one support.

  • The Faith Lab Cohort — a guided group experience for leaders building this capacity together
  • The Faith Lab Circle — an ongoing membership and community of practice for people doing this work
  • Coaching and Advising — one-on-one support for the specific work in front of you
How we work

Start with the architect, not the tools.

If you want to build a house, you don't start with the tools. You start with the architect. The same is true for community. We don't lead with a fixed program; we start with your community as it actually is, then design the experience around it. Most partnerships begin with a single facilitated session, something concrete and contained, and grow from there as trust builds and the work deepens. You don't have to commit to everything to start. You just have to start.
